The Boy's Quiz

This is a fun little brain teaser, or an infuriating nightmare. Your call.

I asked my sons, aged 9 and 7, what they thought were the 10 hardest questions in the world. Only stipulation — the answers must be a whole number. Those 10 questions give us a quiz.

Within 1% — 10 pts  "Right!"
Within 10% — 5 pts  "Close enough"
Within 50% — 2 pts  "Not a terrible ballpark"
Further out — 0 pts

Answer all 10 and score out of 100. The twist: you don't get told the individual answers yet — just your total. Play again and again. Once you crack 90 or more you're put out of your misery and the real numbers revealed. The real test: how many rounds does it take?

P.S. Apparently if there is a tie you have to come to our house and do a two-lap race around the garden. And if you draw that, it's rock paper scissors.
In my 7-year-old's words: "may the luckiest sausage win."

Question 1
How long does it take to make a pair of jeans?
Specifically, the time on the factory assembly line — and even more specifically, a pair of Levi 510s. Answer in minutes, to the nearest whole minute.
Question 2
How many Pokémon cards are there?
Visually distinct, English language Pokémon cards from all runs.
Question 3
How many books are in the British Library?
Not "items" — which includes manuscripts, poems and other random stuff. Actual fully fledged books.
Question 4
How many legs does an average centipede have?
Apparently a common house centipede specifically.
Question 5
How long is a tennis court in centimetres?
Regulation size grass court, baseline to baseline.
Question 6
How many seconds are in a day?
Do you really need help defining this one?
Question 7
How tall was the tallest person in the world, ever?
In centimetres. As per the Guinness Book of World Records.
Question 8
How many branches does an average tree have?
Strictly: the average best guess for how many end-points there are on a fully grown English Oak.
Question 9
How many months did it take to paint the world's biggest painting?
As per Guinness World Records.
Question 10
How many snowflakes are in a snowman?
Big clue: this is just an order of magnitude number — a 1 followed by zeros. Is it 100? 1,000,000? 10,000,000,000,000? Your best guess.
